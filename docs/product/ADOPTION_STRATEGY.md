## Target Client Segments

### Tier 1: High-Volume Med-Mal Defense Firms (Idaho)

**Who**: Firms defending hospitals (St. Luke's, Saint Alphonsus) and physicians in medical malpractice suits.

**Why they need us**:
- Juggling 50+ active cases with overlapping discovery deadlines
- Per Spy: paralegals routinely handle 10+ cases across multiple attorneys simultaneously
- One missed deadline = sanctions, evidence exclusion, or malpractice exposure
- Staff turnover (sick leave, vacations, trials) creates coverage gaps

**Pain points we solve**:
- Automated deadline tracking across all cases
- Warning → deadline → escalation alerts (per Spy's corrected flow)
- Verifiable compliance proofs for court and insurers
- Set number tracking to prevent supplementation numbering errors

**Revenue model**: Per-case subscription or monthly firm license

---

### Tier 2: Solo/Small Plaintiff Med-Mal Firms

**Who**: Plaintiff attorneys (1-5 person firms) handling med-mal cases on contingency.

**Why they need us**:
- No margin for error — one missed deadline can destroy a case worth millions
- Can't afford full-time paralegal staff for discovery tracking
- Often going up against large defense firms with more resources

**Pain points we solve**:
- Level the playing field against well-staffed defense firms
- Automated step generation from rule pack eliminates manual research
- ZK compliance proofs give them credibility with judges

**Revenue model**: Freemium — first case free, then per-case pricing

---

### Tier 3: Insurance Carriers (The Force Multiplier)

**Who**: Medical malpractice insurers — MedPro, TheDoctorsGroup, MIEC (Medical Insurance Exchange of California), and others.

**Why they care**:
- Discovery failures lead to adverse outcomes → increased payouts
- Insured firms with repeated sanctions = higher risk profiles
- Need objective data on insured firms' compliance practices

**What we offer them**:
- **Risk reduction tool** — firms using AD have verifiable compliance records
- **Premium differentiation** — offer discounts to AD-using firms (like safe driver discounts)
- **Claims defense evidence** — "our insured used a verified compliance system"
- **High-risk remediation** — require AD adoption for firms with claims history

**Revenue model**: Enterprise licensing or per-insured-firm fee. Insurance carriers become a **distribution channel** — they incentivize/mandate adoption, firms follow.

**This is potentially the highest-leverage channel.** One insurance carrier partnership could onboard hundreds of firms overnight.

---

### Tier 4: Firms Under Sanctions (The "Probation" Play)

**Who**: Firms that have been sanctioned under IRCP Rule 37 or equivalent for discovery failures.

**Why they're a target**:
- Sanctions are **public record** in Idaho court filings — we can identify these firms
- They have a documented, undeniable pain point
- Their reputation is damaged — they need a visible rehabilitation tool
- Opposing counsel and judges are watching them closely

**Types of sanctions to look for**:
- Motions to compel (opposing counsel forced to ask a judge)
- Evidence exclusion orders
- Adverse inference jury instructions
- Monetary sanctions (attorney fees awarded)
- Case dismissal or default judgment (extreme)

**Adoption path**:
- Approach firm directly: "We noticed [public record]. Here's a tool that prevents this."
- Position AD as reputation rehabilitation, not punishment
- Offer discounted/free pilot to build case study

## Court System Adoption Strategy

### The Judge-First Approach

**Rationale**: If a judge endorses or orders use of a compliance tool, law firms must comply. One judge = instant adoption for all parties in their cases.

### Mechanism: Standing Discovery Order (IRCP Rule 16)

Idaho judges have broad case management authority. A judge could:

1. **Voluntary pilot**: "The court invites parties in complex cases to use verified compliance tracking"
2. **Recommended practice**: "The court recommends parties maintain verifiable discovery compliance records"
3. **Standing order**: "All parties in cases assigned to this division shall maintain compliance tracking"
4. **Remedial order**: After a sanctions event, order a specific firm to use compliance monitoring

### Pitch to Judges & Court Administrators

> *"Your Honor, we'd like to pilot a compliance tracking tool in 5 complex cases this quarter. Both sides get it free. You get fewer discovery motions on your calendar."*

**Value proposition for judges**:
- **Fewer motions to compel** cluttering the docket
- **Objective audit trail** when disputes arise — ZK proof of who did what and when
- **Neutral tool** — proves compliance without revealing privileged strategy
- **Modernization** — demonstrates the court's commitment to technology

### Target: Ada County District Court (Boise)

Start with one friendly judge in Ada County (Idaho's largest, most complex docket). Spy's connections here are critical.

## Network Effect & Competitive Pressure

### The "Arms Race" Dynamic

When one side in a case uses AutoDiscovery:
- They produce **ZK-verified compliance proofs** to the judge
- Opposing counsel looks disorganized by comparison
- The judge starts expecting the same standard from both sides
- Opposing counsel adopts AD for their next case

**This creates organic viral adoption within a jurisdiction.** One firm using AD in a high-profile case triggers adoption by every firm they oppose.

### The Opposing Counsel Playbook

1. Plaintiff firm adopts AD → produces compliance proof at hearing
2. Judge is impressed → asks defense counsel "can you verify your compliance similarly?"
3. Defense counsel can't → adopts AD for future cases
4. Both sides now use AD → judge makes it a standing order
5. Other judges in the courthouse notice → spread to their divisions

### Idaho State Bar Partnership

- **CLE credit program**: "Automated Discovery Compliance in the Digital Age" — attorneys earn continuing education credit while learning AD
- **Best practice endorsement**: Bar recommends AD as a compliance best practice
- **Ethics opinion**: Request an advisory opinion confirming that ZK compliance proofs are acceptable evidence of discovery compliance

### CLE Workshop Content

1. Overview of IRCP discovery obligations
2. Common compliance failures and sanctions
3. Introduction to automated compliance tracking
4. Hands-on: creating a case and generating a compliance proof
5. Court acceptance of digital compliance evidence

**Benefit**: Positions AD as an educational tool, not just software. Builds credibility and awareness simultaneously.
