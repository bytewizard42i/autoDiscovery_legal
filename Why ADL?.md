## Legal Landscape: TAR Case Law Supporting Discovery Tools

The legal landscape strongly supports the use of technology in discovery. Key rulings include:

### Rio Tinto PLC v. Vale S.A., 306 F.R.D. 125 (S.D.N.Y. 2015)

The landmark case endorsing Technology Assisted Review (TAR), holding that:
- **TAR Endorsement:** Courts will generally permit a producing party to use TAR.
- **No Higher Standard:** TAR need only be reasonable — not perfect — and should not be held to a stricter standard than keyword searches or manual review.
- **Transparency:** A TAR protocol requiring transparency between parties was approved.

### Subsequent Key Rulings

| Case | Year | Key Holding |
|---|---|---|
| **Hyles v. New York City** (S.D.N.Y.) | 2016 | A party cannot be forced to use TAR — the responding party controls the review method. |
| **FCA US, LLC v. Cummins, Inc.** (E.D. Mich.) | 2017 | Addressed TAR protocol disputes, validation metrics, and transparency. |
| **City of Rockford v. Mallinckrodt ARD Inc.** (N.D. Ill.) | 2018 | Examined transparency and defensibility standards for TAR. |
| **In re Mercedes-Benz Emissions Litigation** (D.N.J.) | 2020 | Choice of methodology belongs to the producing party. |
| **Livingston v. City of Chicago** (N.D. Ill.) | 2020 | Producing parties are best positioned to decide on methodology. |
| **In re Insulin Pricing Litigation** (D.N.J.) | 2025 | Reinforced transparency and reasonable validation for TAR tools. |

### Trends Favorable to AutoDiscovery

- **Producing party control:** Courts consistently hold that the producing party chooses the review method, including technology-driven solutions.
- **Transparency is key:** Courts expect reasonable disclosure — AutoDiscovery's on-chain attestations provide unmatched transparency.
- **Tool choice is defensible:** As long as the method is reasonable, courts support it — blockchain-verified compliance proofs go above and beyond.
